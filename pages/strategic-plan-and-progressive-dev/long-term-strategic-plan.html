Strategic Plan

How is your club perceived and recognized currently in public?


RACIIT has been chartered for 11 years to this day and has been giving many impactful projects to the society through community service projects. Apart from community services done by RACIIT we also initiate many other fun filled projects along with the aim of developing the youth in a professional perspective which attracts many younger generations to take part in our projects.

RACIIT has been and still is one of the top ten clubs in the district 3220. Projects like HDS, E-Mithudama, Sallys Manor are very popular among the public for a variety of different reasons. Such as the unique and vast impact each project delivers, the individuality between each project, the amount of participation and reach these projects get among the public are some of the reasons why RACIIT is popular among the public. These projects are one of the reasons that has helped to create and build a fine brand image and name that the club perceives now within the district 3220 as well as the public.

Projects like E-Mithudama, a community service project in which we build computer labs for school in the underprivileged areas in the country. E-Mithudama has been initiated for 3 consecutive years and will be running for this year too. E-Mithudama has won the gold award for the most outstanding community service project for 2 consecutive years as well it has won many more awards for its sustainability.

Apart from the community services we initiated HDS, a hotdog eating competition which is one of the major signature projects of RACIIT and also a very popular project among the public. Every year HDS has a major upscale that would reach the potential a project like HDS has. 1000 plus public takes part in the festival because it is one of the most awaited projects expected from our club and the whole package of fun, competitions, stalls, after party, celebrity guest appearances HDS without a fail delivers to the public. HDS won the most outstanding initiative under public relation silver award for the year 2018-2019 and a gold award under the same category for the year 2019-2020 and many more other awards under different categories. Every year HDS has a huge reach among the public and expectation for the brand image this project has created.

RACIIT has not only been popular for the qualitative and quantitative project we give to the community but also for the participation and all the crazy fun filled times we take as a whole club while we are initiating many projects. Along with these RACIIT has won the Most Outstanding Club for the past 3 Consecutive years.

What are your key noteworthy public projects that could sustain?


Hotdog showdown (HDS) is the most significant among the signature events of the Rotaract Club of Informatics Institute of Technology, an initiative under the Public Relations avenue aiming to help fund ‘E-Mithudama’. The project began in 2014, as a basic fund raiser in our very own common room within the IIT premises and has since evolved from being just a hotdog eating competition to one of the most awaited events on the calendar in both the Rotaract community and otherwise.

Each year HDS grows bigger and has now grown to mark its place as the largest Hotdog Eating Competition in South Asia. The event, which was only limited to a Hotdog eating competition, was able to pass beyond its limitations over the past few years and has now marked to be one of the largest food festivals in the country. Apart from this HDS consisted of a dashing motor show, a Massive and a dynamic musical afterparty including many celebrity appearances and performances, a stunt show and an assortment of appetizing food stalls making it an eventful night full of fun and laughter.

Over the last 6 years HDS has grown as a brand that brings a positive public relations effect on its sponsors and partners. The project has also captured a gold and silver award for the Most outstanding public relations initiative in the years 2019 and 2020 respectively, and is now a E-mithudama is a project based around the core objective of helping school children from rural, underprivileged areas of the country- to support and uplift their computer literacy, delivering to the younger generation of our country with the touch of advanced technology that the world is currently experiencing. The project was inspired by the opportunity that could be created by partnering up with Unilever Sri Lanka Limited, the basic ideology of the project being to use laptops and desktops that are left to be dumped, to build “X-Labs” in four underprivileged schools each year.

Once in every 4 years the schools will have their labs renewed with new devices, adding value to the project due to its focus on sustainability. Once the lab is set up, and it is open to be used, sessions on basic computing are conducted- how to use a computer, Microsoft Office etc. so the students are given an introductory path for guidance in their computer literacy. Apart from which a separate session is conducted to the mentors and teachers of these students to get ahead with their teaching and tutoring. Born in the 9

th charter year of the Rotaract Club of IIT, the initiative, has grown to be one of RACIIT’s ground-breaking signature community service projects, a promising project that brings light to different communities all around the nation. The project has added up value to itself by capturing a number of awards starting from when the project was awarded the Runners-up under the category “Winning in Sustainability Development'' at ‘Unilever Sri Lanka Chairman’s Awards 2018’. Shortly after which it was recognized at the 2018-19 Rotaract South Asia multi District Information Organization’s Outstanding Awards, winning a gold award. The project was also awarded the gold award for the most outstanding community service project based on basic education and literacy for two consecutive years 2018-19 and 2019-20. In addition to which it brought home the, “INSPIRE THE FUTURE” service excellence 2018-19 - Gold award, most outstanding project focusing on sustainability for the year 2018-19 - Silver award, most outstanding project focusing on sustainability for the year 2019-20 - Bronze award.

Sallys Manor The halloween season would not be complete without a fantastic Halloween party to cap it off. RACIIT as the veterans of this tradition hosts Sally’s Manor, an annual halloween party, in which all the members are involved in carrying out the tasks of the project. Starting o with the initial planning to the actors of the horror house, decoration and stall in charges are all our very own members.

The event comprises the biggest horror house in the whole island and is followed by DJ party alongside stalls such as food and face painting. In 2019 Sally's Manor was held for the third consecutive year at IIT premises. Being one of the large-scale Halloween parties happening in the island, Sally’s Manor had a turnout of over 900 attendees during the last year and was awarded as one of the most Outstanding fundraisers for community service. Sally’s Manor not only holds the pride of being one of the ways to raise funds for our signature community service projects but is also a chance for everyone to mingle and meet new people along with our own newly joined members who had the chance to enjoy themselves. Trailblazers is one of the signature projects initiated by Rotaract Club of Informatics Institute of Technology under the Professional Development avenue. Trailblazers has been initiated in the year 2017 and has been conducted for 3 consecutive years and is one of the most expected projects under the PD avenue. Trailblazers has been partnered up with many Interact clubs for the past 3 years.

Trailblazers pursues the theme of leadership, teamwork, and entrepreneurship, which endures the goal of being a guiding light for the youth to reinforce their personalities and inspire themselves through interactions and experience they will value immensely in the long run, and together to be a vast community of dedicated learners, leaders and entrepreneurs who will be capable of reaching towards the climax in the trail to success.

Each year Trailblazers ups its scale in terms of impact and participation along with Interact clubs and covers a vast area of topics in terms of its theme.

In the year 2019-2020 trailblazers happened from the month of April to the month of May, but due to the pandemic the project had to be moved into the virtual platform, we went on Instagram live rather than be conducted physically as we initially planned.

Yasas Hewage, Fahad Farook, Peter D`Almeida, Bernandine Jayasinghe, Gaia Kodithuwakku, Manuranga Wijesekara, and Bathiya Jayakody discussing their life experience along with providing the participants immense knowledge about many different topics of Entrepreneurship, Leadership and Teamwork we were able to gather 5500 plus participants with the help of such phenomenal speakers. We would not be RACIIT if entertainment and fun is not included in our projects, so as usual we went on full blast with the team Rubber band with 4 multi talented individuals who made the participants wish they were there physically with the amount of entertainment and fun they provided through instagram live. We concluded the project with lots of discussion, immense amounts of knowledge, entertainment and laughter.

We proudly present Trailblazers which won the Silver Award for the Most Outstanding Project with Interact Clubs and many more awards.

IGF the current generation doesn’t see gaming as a hobby but more of a passion and the Rotaract Club of IIT wanted to showcase that to everyone else by our very own initiative IIT GameFest also known as “IGF”. As eSports is up and coming in Sri Lankathese days and is recognized as a sport by the government, IGF is another esports competition that paves the way for gamers around Sri Lanka along with others. RACIIT, being a club of an IT based institute, initiated "IGF'' back in 2016 in order to give a chance for youth to engage and experience this category of sports.

IIT GameFest took place for four consecutive years and was taken to its highest peak over the years. With the development of the technological areas of graphics the games keep getting more detailed and addictive, the game fest consisted of Dota 2, Call of Duty 4, FIFA 19, Mobile Legends, Fortnite and PUBG Mobile. The biggest and trendiest games are showcased with professional gamers as well as kids and adults from all over the island.

Last year IG was a ranked tournament making it one of the biggest projects of the club during the last year and proudly bears ranking as the 3rd best Gaming Tournament in Sri Lanka. HaXmas is the signature project of the Information Technology avenue, which is a small ideathon combined with a startup conference followed by an innovation festival , organized with the aim of bringing like-minded people with innovative ideas together. This was initiated with the sole purpose of creating a platform which would allow young minds to present their ideas for the numerous problems faced by the present day society. This has been receiving recognition from the latest emerging companies and has been taking place for three consecutive years.

The first phase of project HaXmas was a month-long project which consisted of an Initial pitch, mentor session and a final pitch.

Over 50+ teams from universities and institutes from all over the country, each containing up to 4 members registered by submitting a Proof of Concept(PoC) which contained a document containing a brief explanation of their project along with its sustainability. These project proposals were presented to a panel of Judges consisting of qualified lecturers from IIT who choose the 20 teams qualifying for the Initial pitch. The Initial pitch which was held on the 19 th of January 2020 at StartUpX Foundry started off without a hitch as the participants presented their ideas to the judges.

This took place as a closed pitch where only the judges would be present during the pitch. Once the pitching was over, we moved onto the mentor session in which we provided each team with individual attention from the judges. Further on, the innovation festival choosed 3 teams who will receive support from our partners to go forward with their products, this included cash prizes as well as resources to provide the young innovators with the invigoration they need. This project was a massive success and provided a greater understanding on entrepreneurship and created a platform which will bring forth a new generation of successful and innovative youth. Seva “Service Across Borders” , is the signature international services project, done by the Rotaract Club of IIT to host and collaborate with foreign rotaractors to conduct community services projects that will uplift the community. For this purpose, the Rotaract Club of IIT hosted a group of Hong Kong and Australian rotaractors for a week to conduct community service projects while going on tours with them in Sri Lanka.

The first activity was a project named Game of Life which took place at the Independence Square, Colombo.The purpose of this project was to let each team complete community service projects as they possibly could within a specified time period. The next Project was a sports project where the rotaractors from both clubs got the opportunity to showcase their athleticism by playing Futsal on the same day. The next day we all participated in a beach clean-up project in Wellawatta area to show our gratitude to the environment and keep our surroundings clean. We organized this as an awareness project to educate young citizens, who are gradually maturing into dominant adults and to demonstrate them the importance of respecting the environment as well as how a simple act today is capable of contributing to a better world tomorrow.

We had another community service project in Kandy where the two clubs joined hands with each other to conduct an interactive session for the students at Naranvita Kanishta Vidyalaya in Kandy. Moreover, stationeries were donated to the students at the end of the session. Upon completing this project we took our fellow rotaractors from Hong Kong and Australia on a hike to Hanthana mountain range which is one of the famous and most scenic hiking trails in Sri Lanka.As a part of the animal protection program, we visited Ahungalla Turtle Hatchery Centre to provide a contribution in saving the endangered turtle species. There, we helped in numerous activities of this center, such as feeding, cleaning and giving medicine to sick turtles.SEVA 2.0 was successfully completed and we will be doing SEVA 3.O definitely this time as well , if the covid 19 situation in the country settles down .

What goals do you intend to achieve as a club by 2023?


We started off this year with many goals for the club in terms of improving and taking the club to greater heights and length. “Alone we can do so little and together we can do so much more” and we intend to do so much more together as a team in improving the club and achieving all the goals that we put for ourselves as well as all the Citations and District goals that are given to us. RACIIT has always been eager to try and achieve all the goals that were given to us.

As for this year there are 6 District Goals that we are aiming to fulfill.

  1. Covid - 19 Prevention. Project Epitome was an initiative under the Community Service Avenue taken to achieve the goal of Covid - 19 prevention, due to the pandemic situation in the country. Epitome is an ongoing project to this day. The first phase of the project was a “Food Drive”, where our board and members donated and collected those food to give to homeless people and low income workers working in the street as they were impacted mostly due to the pandemic and the whole lockdown situation. The Second phase of the project was “Rakawaranaya” in which we partnered up with Office.lk and donated Facility cleaners and hand wash for more than 50 underprivileged schools. Third phase of the project was a blood donation campaign conducted to donate blood to the blood banks as they were running out of blood and were in need of it due to corona. We successfully concluded all the three phases of the project Epitome and are still working towards in the movement of preventing Covid-19 and creating awareness about the pandemic in the society.
  2. Mental - well being. Project Wevolve initiated under Professional Development Avenue was mainly focused on the topic mental health and well being. Project Wevolve was carried throughout the month of August, where we covered topics such as, “Mental well being, Suicidal thoughts, Child Abuse, and Domestic Abuse” with 4 diffrent phenomenal speakers conducted 4 sessions in educating the participants the and giving them the full insight about these topics and how important it is to come forward and speak if anyone is going through any of these problems. We completed the project by delivering the desired goal.
  3. Peace and Communal Harmony. Project Engal Sri Lanka was initiated and conducted for the past 2 years. And we are planning to continue and conduct the project this year too. Engal Sri Lanka was an initiative under the Community Service Avenue to spread peace and educate cultural awareness among the public. We implemented this project by visiting the cultural places of different religions and educating the participants the importance of that culture and said religion. The main goal of this project was and is to spread peace and have a communal harmony between the races and religions in our country by providing an insight to everyone the importance and the value of each religion is to that community. By carrying out this project we were able to observe many younger generations kept an open mind about other religions and cultures as well as they started respecting other religions and cultures among their peers.
  4. Educational and Sustainable Livelihoods. Project E-Mithudama is an initiative under the Community Service Avenue and has been conducted for the past 2 consecutive years, and we plan to carry out the legacy of e-mithudama in the upcoming years without a doubt. This project is about in which we build computer labs for school in the underprivileged areas in the country. Our main objective is of helping school children from rural, underprivileged areas of the country- to support and uplift their computer literacy, delivering to the younger generation of our country with the touch of advanced technology that the world is currently experiencing. Upto this day we have built and provided 7 labs to the underprivileged schools in the rural area of our country, and we have planned to provide more computers and provide a sustainable education to the students through this project.

    Project Back to school was an initiative under Community Service Avenue for this year. This is a project where we focus on the sustainability of making all the students learn. There are a huge number of people who do not attend school due to certain reasons, and there are also students who have missed their lessons due to the pandemic and the lockdown situations. We initiated this project with the aim of educating and teaching the important subjects from grade 6 onwards. We are planning to achieve this goal through implementing this project by making recordings of all the chapter and term papers with the attachment of soft copies. Project Back to School is right now an ongoing project.
  5. Environmental and Green Life. Project We-Plant is an initiative under the Community Service Avenue happening for the 2nd consecutive year. Our main objective of this project is to create an awareness about the importance of Environmental and Green life and also to take the step to try and protect green life as our world is moving towards destroying green life due to the amount of carbon emitted and global warming. Phase I of this project was carried out by collecting a large number of videos of people planting trees and plants in the home garden. The motive behind collecting videos was to create an awareness about green life and provide an opportunity for the younger generations to take the first step and plant a tree to protect green life. As the Phase II we are planning to plant 200 mangroves which is a habitat for certain types of reef fishes, birds and some herbivore animals. It also helps to prevent soil erosion. We Plant is right now an ongoing project.
  6. Fellowship and Membership Development. Project Truth or dare 2.0 was an initiative under the club service avenue solely based on fellowship and membership development as it was open not only to RACIITians but members of other clubs as well. The very first edition of truth or dare held in July which was open to only our club members motivated us to extend it to other clubs due to the impactful outcome we received. Truth or dare 2.0 which was a Halloween based competition, was held in the month of November, and had a turnout of 30+ teams and as a result had to be extended to another day. It was a highly interactive session as the project committee who gave the tasks to be done and to be said had to interact throughout the round. We received tons of good feedback from the teams that participated and were overjoyed to know that they are eagerly waiting until next time. Project Introduction to RACIIT was an initiative under Club service Avenue. As for the constitution of our club it is mandatory for a prospective member to carry out a micro project and attend 3 general meetings in order to be a general member. However, this

    year we were unable to carry out meetings and micro projects physically and as a result came up with a new initiative of a workshop displaying as to how we function as a club and what our vision and mission is to the members of the new intake. Introduction to RACIIT was held in the month of November and all the prospective members who took part left with all their doubts been met. Individually, all the avenues gave a presentation on what they do and encouraged our newbies. This project also bought in more new members who were retested. In addition, we carried out a survey to be filled by our general members on what should be improved in our projects.

    We are planning to carry out more impactful projects to fulfill and achieve the District goals while focussing on the sustainability of our projects to leave a major impact in the society and help the society out.

What partnerships and sponsorships would benefit your club’s project finances?


Organizations who are willing to engage in a partnership generally don’t handle things according to trial and error, they look for a bearable risk that will ensure long term growth which benefits their company or organization.
