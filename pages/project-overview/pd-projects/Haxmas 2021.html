HaXmas

A hackathon is a design sprint-like event, most often, in which programmers and experts are involved in software development. The goal of a hackathon is to create a functioning prototype by the end of the event. Additionally, hackathons are proving grounds for innovation to arise. They also aim to stimulate the creative and problem-solving juices of developers.

Haxmas is known to be the biggest Hackathon which is organized by a Rotaract Club . For the 4th consecutive year, the Rotaract Club of IIT organized Project Haxmas. Haxmas 2021 was a combination of two hackathons and ideathon to bring out fresh talent as participants brainstormed solutions to various problems based on the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals.

Phase 1- Parallax (UI/UX Hackathon)

Parallax is the first-ever UI/UX virtual 24-hour hackathon organized by a Rotaract club in Sri Lanka. It was open to all undergraduates from across Sri Lanka and we managed to receive 40+ registrations. Owing to the strict movement restrictions, the hackathon which was held on the 24th of October initiated at 9.00 am and completed at 9.00 am the following day. Every team had 3 to 6 members and the requirement was to design a website for the Lanka premier league, incorporating the theme of the Lanka premier league. Within the first 8 hours, they were requested to submit the design of the UI and the next few hours were spent on developing the website. The winners of Parallax were awarded with following prizes.

  • Winners - Rs.15,000.
  • First Runners up- Rs. 10,000
  • Second Runners up - Parallax Goodies
Phase 2 Haxmas 2021(Hackthon and Ideathon)- Haxmas aimed to bring together teams of undergraduates to devise technological solutions to at least one out of the SDG goals. They then worked on developing and implementing their idea assisted by feedback from three successful mentors of the industry.

The competition requested teams of 3 to 6 members preparing an excellent proposal detailing their plans for an app or website which addresses their technological solution using the provided proposal template. Having the shortlisted teams announced, the eligible teams spent the next one month on the development of their proposed project, during which they had weekly mentoring sessions to receive feedback on their levels of progress and development.

Finally, the teams were judged based on a set of criteria which including but not limited to feasibility, creativity and design by distinguished persons from the industry; Ms. Anna Kalm, CEO of Ascentic, Ms. Dhanisha Paul, Director of Delivery at Sysco Labs and Mr. Prabath Chamika, Head of Development at IronClad. The finals and awards ceremony took place physically at Likuid Spaces, with a limited and controlled crowd, adhering to the Covid-19 regulations.

The winners of Haxmas were awarded with following prizes.
  • Winners - Rs.50,000.
  • First Runners up- Rs. 20,000
  • Second Runners up - Hax Goodies .
Both the award ceremonies were held on the same day after the final pitch .Owing to the current situation of the country only the top 10 teams of the phase 2 and winners of the parallax were asked to join the physical session along with the committee. This was held on the 17th of April 2021. All arrangements were made for the winners to collect their cash prizes and were appreciated for their incredible, skillful work.

By attaining over 40 teams, consisting of 116 members for phase 1 and 50 registrations and being able to reach out to over 1000+ youngsters in phase 2 of Haxmas 2021 , we believe HaXmas created a large impact amongst the IT and developing community. With the theme of phase 2 HaXmas ’21 being the Sustainable Development Goals, where participants developed technological solutions based on one or more Goals, the projects developed contribute to bringing us closer to achieving the SDGs. With so many teams innovating for a better future, the positive impact is undeniable.
