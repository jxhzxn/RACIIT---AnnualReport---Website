Fierce 3.0

The ability to protect oneself from harm is a basic human right that should be accessible to everyone. Teaching young people self-defense is something we lack in today’s society, but it is of utmost importance. Most people cannot even imagine getting attacked, much less expect it to happen when they go out on the street. Unfortunately, these types of attacks occur daily in our country and across the globe, so it is always best that you know how to defend yourself, both physically and verbally.
“FIERCE 3.0” was an initiative under the Professional Development Avenue in collaboration with the Interact Club of Visahka Vidyalaya to educate young children on the importance and basics of self-defense. ‘FIERCE 3.0’ was conducted at the Royal College Sports Complex on the days of 3rd and 4th of October 2020. Along with self-defense we achieved to improve the well-being of the participants in this event. And by improving the well-being of a large number of participants we could confidently say that this younger generation will be an example and also improve the well-being of the society. After all, aren’t we the next generation of this community?
Teaching today’s youth proper ways to defend themselves from harm will instill a confidence in them that comes from within, when they trust and rely on themselves for the responsibility of their own safety and welfare as well the faith that they can take care of others as well. Self-defense helps to prepare you for unexpected situations and also helps develop increased mental and physical strength.

Project Impact

  • Build the confidence among the young children to defend themselves through a series of self-defense sessions.
  • Teach various techniques that can be adhered to defend oneself and those around , involving different scenarios of attack.
  • Improve the well-being of the young children to set themselves as an example in maintaining the well being of the society.
