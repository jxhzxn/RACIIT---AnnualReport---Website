E-Mithudama

“E-Mithudama” is a community service project based around the core objective of helping school children from rural, underprivileged areas of the country- to support and uplift their computer literacy, delivering to the younger generation of our country with the touch of advance technology that the world is currently experiencing. The initiative was born in the 9th charter year of the Rotaract Club of IIT, since which, it has grown to be one of RACIIT’s ground-breaking signature community service projects, a promising project that brings light to different communities all around the nation, so on a day in future we can raise ourselves up as one, cherishing on the impact we have brought for others.
The basic ideology of the project being to use laptops and desktops that are left to be dumped, to build “X-Labs” in four underprivileged schools each year this year the project expanded to six chosen schools due to the opportunity created by receiving computers from Uniliver Sri Lanka and Standard Chartered. The Project was completed on the 31st of May under the secondary avenue of Educational and sustainable livelihoods, taking the “X-Labs” journey to the Debarawewa Central College, Beliatta Maha Vidyala and Dehigahalanda Maha Vidyalaya from the Hambantota District, Badanagoda junior school Aluthgama, Vishwaloka Vidayalya and Dhanajaya maha Vidyalaya, making an impact on thousands of young lives that attend the schools from various grades by giving them the equal opportunity like the other students coming from privileged schools and areas all over Sri Lanka to educate themselves in the ever growing field of Information Technology and pass out successfully. This year we aimed to provide each lab with 10 to 15 computers and its relevant equipment.
Due to the pandemic, E-learning increased in popularity, as many students focused on developing their education through online classes. Hence the idea of “Locked Learn” was born into the initiative, we aim to be uploading all the necessary courses related to IT work for students looking for courses in the related subjects and also hope to provide free courses that the students can complete by themselves and grow forward with technology.
Project impact ● Creating an opportunity for underprivileged students to experience technological advancements in addition to introducing a self-learning environment for the students. ● The greatest way a project can leave its mark in a community is by how sustainable and enduring it is and the concept of “X-labs” secures this idea. It focuses on the sustainable development goals, Quality Education, Affordable and Clean Energy and Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure. ● Teachers were provided with the opportunity to broaden their knowledge and work on their teaching skills as IT is a changing field that requires a person learning it or especially teaching it to be up to date. ● Once the devices are placed in the lab environments, they are then refurbished into an environment that promotes self-learning. Setting up the labs is only a phase of a process once each lab is completed we will be visiting these schools every four years, seeing to their necessities and upgrading the labs. Thus, conveying sustainability as promised. Secondary avenue- Education Chairperson - Amesha Vithanage Sdg goals- 04. Quality Education,07. Affordable and clean energy,09.
