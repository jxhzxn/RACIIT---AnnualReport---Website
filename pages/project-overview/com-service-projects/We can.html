WeCan

Menstruation is a concern because it has always been clouded in misconceptions that have perpetuated over generations. In many parts of the world, predominantly due to cultural discrepancies and detrimental concepts that have been taken into consideration, the concept of menstruation is highly stigmatized. The Rotaract club of IIT, identified the need to change the narrative and set on its journey to normalize PERIODS with the initiative “WeCan”.
The first phase of the project, which was a series of online sessions concluded in December 2020, raising awareness about menstruation through online awareness sessions about menstruation, menstruation cups and reusable pads, conducted by doctors and advocates to speak about menstruation and to solve the common questions most women have.
As for the second phase we aimed to bust the myths and break the stigma around menstruation via a social media campaign that included numerous recognized young men and women that shared their thoughts on menstruation, period poverty, tax and the pollution it causes. Alongside which we also conducted awareness sessions in three selected rural underprivileged schools educating over 600 students in total, with the help of speakers who will be talking about the safety of menstrual cups and how we can use it. We started off with the Sacred heart convent, Galle which took place on the 6th of April 2021. The next call was to Gnanissara Maha Vidyalaya, Aluthgama, on the 21st of April 2021 and the Badanagoda junior school on 22nd April 2021.
Looking forward to the world Menstrual Hygiene day, which falls on the 28th May, we began the “Red dot challenge” as the third phase of the project. A campaign aiming to include numerous young men and women to share their support on the journey to normalize talking about menstruation. Together with us, over 500 young men and women, from around the island, gave voice to "Break the Stigma". Additionally, in honour of the Menstrual Hygiene Day we intended to take another step forward to combat period poverty in Sri Lanka and hence donated 25 sets of reusable sanitary pads, from Momiji Natural, to girls from low income families to help them have safe hygienic menstruation. Project impact ● Raising awareness on Menstrual hygiene and giving young girls from around the island to be educated on the seriousness of the topic, planting the depth and importance of the subject being addressed by making them aware of the various health and environmental impacts it holds. ● provided proper guidance to maintain this hygiene and give girls the opportunity to clarify their questions on the subject, that they most likely found hard to express to a parent or teacher they meet on the daily. ● Educate numerous men and women on the importance of breaking the stigma revolving around the subject proving that leaving menstruation as an inconsequential topic doesn't just affect mentrualtors but also the environment. ● By focusing on educating the men of the society, we were able to eliminate the ideology of menstrual shame. ● By promoting the use of reusable sanitary products we not only made aware of the options that one can take towards responsible consumption, we also promoted the importance of fusing greener alternatives. ● donated reusable pads that are enough to cover an entire menstrual cycle, that can be used for about five years, considering proper usage Secondary avenue- Mental Health Chairperson- Akarashani Ramanayake, Kirithika Senathiraja Start Date -2020-10-07 End Date -2021-05-28 SDG goals- 03.
