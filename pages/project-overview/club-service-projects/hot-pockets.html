Hot Pockets

Pool, a game belonging under the classification of cue sports, is played on a table with six pockets along the rails, into which balls are deposited, is one of the most popular indoor games that requires many skills such as determination, attentiveness, precision, and vigilance.

On the 19th of September 2020, the Rotaract club of Informatics institute of Technology along with the Rotaract Club of Colombo Fort carried out Hot pockets 2.0 for the second consecutive year at the stripes pool parlor, Wellawatte.

The tournament commenced at 10.00am following the arrival of all the participants and the draws were made by creating 8 draws consisting of 8 people in each draw. Participants were also given the opportunity to re-enter if they desired. This year we received over 35 entries and the rest of the players re-entered. This tournament was open to Rotaractors, rotarians and anyone who wished to participate.

After announcing and awarding the winners with trophies for the winners, the event was brought to an end at around 11.00pm and was concluded making yet another successful initiative under the club service avenue which also paved way to brand new friendships and memories that are bound to last a lifetime. Moreover, they say two are better than one, this project was none other than a simple representation of the things you can achieve by doing a joint project or in other words, working together as one in fellowship and harmony.

Project Impact

  • Give a chance for people to come and enjoy themselves after a tiresome week of hard work. We accompanied a crowd of about 60-70, including all the participants.
  • We bonded together as we had to make decisions together, face challenges together, etc. The long-term outcome would be making friends and strengthening the bonds of the existing ones.
