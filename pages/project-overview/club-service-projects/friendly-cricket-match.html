Friendly Cricket match - RAC ICBT & RACIIT

“Cricket is a team game. If you want fame for yourself, go play an individual game.” -Gautam Gambhir
This popular sport which originated in England is now widely popular in countries such as India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Australia, the West Indies, and South Africa. Apart from all the physical benefits, the game of cricket also brings about many other benefits.One of the main reasons for this sport to be widely popular is that the game builds team spirit and owing to the ability of being able to set it up anywhere with just the availability of a bat and a ball, almost every young boy and sometimes even girls learn to play this game. If you are a Sri Lankan, the game of cricket is not one that should be taught but instead is one that runs in the blood.
The Rotaract clubs of IIT along with ICBT put together a friendly game of cricket merely just to strengthen the bond between the members of the club. The match took place on the 24th of January 2021 at CFC grandstand, Dehiwala. The tournament was limited to just 11 players per team and a few spectators along with the referees to keep score. Owing to the current situation in the country, we also had to ensure we adhere to the health protocols and as a result limited the number of spectators and kept it open only for club members. Project Impact ● Just as most friendly matches are organized to promote fellowship, this was accomplished, not just on the event day, but also in planning and coordinating. ● We were also able to develop people skills such as interacting and making new connections, which requires people skills. ● Creating new bonds amongst the members.
