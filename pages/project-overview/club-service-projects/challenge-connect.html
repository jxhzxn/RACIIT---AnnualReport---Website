Challenge : Connect

With the outbreak of COVID-19, almost all our day-to-day activities were transferred to virtual platforms. Including, universities resuming virtually. Although this may seem convenient and efficient at first, there are many drawbacks that come along with it later.

The Rotaract movement is one that encourages young people to engage in community service and uplift others whilst also developing themselves. Self-improvement varies from person to person and is often achieved gradually over time and the Rotaract Club of IIT is a place that brings out the best in a person, giving them the chance to find their place in the corporate world. Therefore, with the main aim of creating an initiative to bring out the best in our members whilst also fulfilling the aim of membership retention we introduced project Challenge: Connect.

Challenge: connect was a four day-long online series of games, ice breakers and challenges. The project which started on the 17th of April was completed by the 21st, bringing in brand new friendships within the members of the club. The project started off by creating WhatsApp groups and was allocated two to three mandatory tasks and an optional task every day. We had a total 20 groups, with each consisting of 10 to 11 members and an overall participation of 213 members.

The project gave an outcome even better than expected and the feedback received was absolutely rewarding to know that as for a project under the club service avenue we have not only contributed towards the aspect of member retention, but also elevated the value of fellowship.



Project Impact

  • Despite the fact that the project was mainly oriented towards exclusively RACIITians, as days passed, we also discovered that as directors, we too were learning and discovering things about our members which we never knew. Just as it brought joy to our eyes to see them making new friends and discovering themselves, it also helped us find new friendships.
  • Helped us create new friendships and gave a better understanding about our fellow members. Which in return gives us the ability to help them find their personal interests in the club.
  • With the Rotaract year almost drawing to end, our main focus was to lay the foundation for the future of the club, in other words, member retention could be accomplished just by obtaining aid from a few techniques such as this.
  • We discovered that this project had reached out and impacted almost every participant, making it yet another great club service initiative by the Rotaract Club of IIT.
