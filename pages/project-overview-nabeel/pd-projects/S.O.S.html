S.O.S

The "Rule of Threes" provides a guideline of how to prioritize basic survival skills: first shelter, then water, and lastly food. Surviving a difficult wilderness situation also requires meeting many challenges while avoiding panic. Teamwork teaches essential communication and social skills, such as active listening and effective speaking. It is important for students to recognize that speaking is not the same as talking. Leadership helps you communicate in both directions. Effective leadership involves working with people's motivations, which you can only learn from other people.

The Professional Development Avenue of The Rotaract Club of IIT initiated the first step in improving, educating, and developing basic survival skills, leadership skills, team building skills, critical thinking, creativity, thinking out of the box, problem-solving, communication and listening skills for the participants through various fun and adventure games held at Che Adventure in Hanwella on 21st of February 2021, to make it more fun and interactive while also to give them an exposure as well as experience after the long period of lockdown. Throughout all the adventure activities they had lots of fun while gaining an immense amount of knowledge, experience, and developing various aspects of life within 1 day.

The main goal of project S.O.S was to develop the above-mentioned skills for all the 80 participants who took part in this project with anticipation and eagerness to indulge and learn something new.

We successfully concluded the project and delightfully observed at the end of the day that all the objectives and importance we taught them had been well grasped by each and every one in their own unique way. Every participant who attended the project learnt many or at least one lesson, realized the importance of life, developed themselves while having fun and making memories before they returned home and that was the success of S.O.S. Certificate of Participation was provided for everyone.
