We Plant

It is known that trees provide 80% of the world’s terrestrial biodiversity, trees help clean the very air we breathe, filter the water we drink, absorb harmful chemicals, supply medicine for our bodies and even provide over 1.6 billion jobs to people like you and me. Devoting to the sentimental value these treasures bring, the Rotaract Club of IIT initiated ‘We plant’ to restore the damage caused to trees due to our very own inhuman carelessness and take a bigger step towards protecting the future of the planet.

Phase 01 was fixated on home gardening and domestic plants, starting from our very own members taking the first step. To promote this very idea, we requested people to plant a minimum of one plant and submit proof of them doing so. This phase which began on the 27th of October was completed by the 10th of November.

The second phase took place in Chilaw at Anawilundawa wetland sanctuary on the 26th of January, 4th February and on the 28th of February, respectively. Given the situation of the country, we had to carry this phase with the cooperation and support from the government authorities such as the department of Forestry and planted over 1500 mangroves within the wetlands in Chillaw. Despite the convenience of attaining land under a government body with only the inconvenience of receiving a guaranteed approval, we had to start the planting process from scratch.

In phase 3, we decided to cultivate such lands with economically beneficial plants such as coconut, banana, manioc and a few other medicinal plant species. In order to look after the plantations, we decided to find a few low-income families. Therefore, through this we were able to provide them with a mode of income (by selling the harvest) for a specific period.

Seagrass meadows are also known as one of the most important ecosystems on the planet as it aids in the overall health of the ocean. Therefore, as phase 4 we initiated the plantation of seagrass in Seenigama, and Kapparathota areas, hoping they provide food and shelter to marine species ranging from large dugongs, turtles and sharks to small sea horses and shrimps.
