Voice for the Voiceless

Animals cannot tell their stories of suffering. Together, we can end animal cruelty. We can end this by loudly calling our cruelty and influencing governments and businesses to change their processes. Together, we can give all animals a life worth living. Together, we must be their voice.

The Rotaract Club of IIT proposed the project named “Voice for the Voiceless” which has been continuing for the past six years straight as a community service project.But, this year because of the immense need in being a voice to the voiceless we wanted to initiate and collaborate with those beyond boundaries and continue this project as an international services project.

This year's project Voice for the Voiceless, not only focused on every animal but conducted a total of four phases, each where it was a very active, involved and yet again sustainable phase.

Project Voice for the Voiceless includes a total of four phases, 1. Donation for animal care and protection centers, doing relevant vaccinations. 2. Conducting street animals feeding sessions. 3. Spreading awareness on animal cruelty and abuse. 4. Distributing neon collars for the strays. Rtr. Dinuri Vitharana and Rtr.Levhara Gunawardena were the Co-chairpersons for this project and along with the committee, was able to make sure that we have the ability to protect not all, but so many stray lives and animals out there who need our care and love. We achieved this from the sessions we had with the reputed animal care organizations in the months of January, February and March.

Along with the collaboration of foreign clubs, we conducted feeding sessions to feed many stray lives in the streets and as well as in the wild .We distributed collars to make sure that the stray dogs could be seen during the night time which makes the project very valuable. The project also hopes to reduce the number of insecurities seen each year by humans doing insensitive damage related to animals that can be treated if detected earlier.We were also able to raise a voice through the social media campaign we conducted. Thus, this project hopes to change this situation. From this project, we got connected with the Rotaract Clubs from foreign countries for this worthy course.
