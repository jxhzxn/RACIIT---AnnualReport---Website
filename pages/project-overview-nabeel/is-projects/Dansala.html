DANSALA

The project “Dansala” is the most significant international services project, initiated by the Rotaract Club of IIT, which concluded for the 4th consecutive year. In the first year of its initiation in the year 2017, we could parallelly conduct it only with a few participations of foreign Rotaract clubs, but this year Dansala was successfully concluded, reaching a milestone of 51 Rotaract Clubs of 40 Rotaract Districts in 30 countries of 5 continents.

Moving into details about, “What exactly is project “Dansala” all about? As Sri Lankans, we are very much aware of the famous Buddhist Festival named “Vesak”, which is annually celebrated in Sri Lanka, in memory of three momentous happenings in the life of Lord Buddha. Under this festival, the Sri Lankan Buddhists conduct a special practice of selfless giving, known by Sri Lankans, as “Dansala”.

We invited many foreign clubs from across the world to join us with this great initiative. As a result, we got many replies from many foreign clubs around the world, appreciating our work in helping global communities in need, and they were very happy to be a part of this project. We had many foreign Rotaract clubs from many foreign countries such as India, Pakistan, Nepal, Italy, Senegal, Tanzania, Philippines, Sierra Leone, Bangladesh, Mexico, Tunisia, Guyana, Dominican Republic, and many other countries representing different continents of the world by participating in this project.

Therefore, under this international service initiative, we were able to reach out to people in need globally, in these unfortunate times. Most importantly, realizing the fact that this is the time where these people need our help the most, regardless of the Covid 19 pandemic situation, we strived to help these communities and people in need with the spread of covid 19, charity and giving back have taken on a whole new importance. Project “Dansala” is not just about what you donate, it is about what you do, and how you show empathy to all living beings.
